icon: Book
---

Clippkit was created to make it easier **_(and way more fun)_** to build high-quality, animated videos on the web.

It's is a growing set of reusable building blocks for video. Scenes, effects, and components you can mix, match, and extend however you like. Built to save time, stay consistent, and help you focus on what actually matters: making great videos with code.

Whether you're working on content for YouTube, TikTok, product demos, or full-blown video editors. Clippkit gives you a head start with components that actually look good, animate smoothly, and follow a consistent structure.

This is a project by and for developers who want to create video content using code, and we’re hoping it grows into something the whole community can build on.

---

### 💡 Why use it

- You’re tired of copy-pasting the same motion logic into your Remotion projects.
- You want components that look good out of the box but are still fully customizable.
- You care about structure, reusability, and clean code - not timelines in After Effects.
- You’re building video products, short-form content, or visual storytelling tools with React.
- You want to treat video scenes the same way you treat UI - with design systems, consistency, and reusability.

---

### 🧩 How it works

Clippkit components are just React components. You import them into your Remotion composition, pass in your content (text, images, video paths), and you're ready to go. Everything is built with customization in mind - from timing and delay props to animation styles and layout overrides.

Use them as-is, or fork and tweak to make your own design system for video.

---

### 🌍 Our vision

We’re building Clippkit to be open-source, collaborative, and creatively unopinionated.

We want it to feel like a design system - but for motion. A library of styled video components that are accessible, composable, and easy to remix.

We’re hoping other developers join in: contribute new components, suggest ideas, or fork it to fit your own style. Let’s push forward what’s possible with video on the web.
